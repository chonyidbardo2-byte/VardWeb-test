Accessibility
Statement

We are committed to building an inclusive web experience that works for everyone — regardless of ability, device, or assistive technology.

Last updated: 19 June 2026 — Full AA conformance

// 01 — our commitment

Built for everyone. No exceptions.

VardWeb is committed to ensuring that our websites — vardweb.ca and vardweb.com — are accessible to all people, including those with visual, auditory, motor, or cognitive disabilities. Accessibility is not a feature we add at the end — it is a design principle we build in from the start.

We target conformance with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA, as published by the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C). We are also guided by Ontario's Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act (AODA), including the Integrated Accessibility Standards Regulation (IASR).

Accessibility is an ongoing commitment. We continuously evaluate and improve our sites based on user feedback, automated audits, and manual testing. This document is a living record of that effort.

// 02 — conformance status

Where we stand today.

📋

Conformance Status: Conformant — WCAG 2.1 Level AA. Our sites conform to WCAG 2.1 Level AA. All Level A and Level AA success criteria are met as of June 2026. No known gaps remain.

We have implemented all WCAG 2.1 Level A and AA success criteria across all public pages. The standard referenced is the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) version 2.1, published by the W3C Web Accessibility Initiative (WAI). A full criterion-by-criterion breakdown is available in our WCAG Compliance Report.

// 03 — technical specifications

The technologies our sites rely on.

Our sites are built with the following web technologies, each of which is relied upon for accessibility conformance:

  • HTML5 — Semantic markup including landmarks (nav, main, footer), heading hierarchy, and descriptive link text.
  • CSS3 — Responsive layout, focus-visible states on all interactive elements, print styles, and sufficient colour contrast ratios.
  • WAI-ARIA — ARIA roles, states, and properties on custom interactive components including the mobile navigation accordion (aria-expanded, aria-controls) and navigation buttons (aria-label).
  • JavaScript (ES Modules) — Used for progressive enhancement only. Core content and navigation remain accessible without JavaScript.

// 05 — keyboard navigation

Fully navigable without a mouse.

Our sites are designed to be fully operable via keyboard alone. A "Skip to main content" link appears on the first Tab keypress on every page, allowing keyboard users to bypass repeated navigation blocks directly. The following additional accessibility features are implemented:

  • Skip link — A visually hidden "Skip to main content" link is the first focusable element on every page. Appears on Tab keypress, moves focus directly to main content.
  • Tab / Shift+Tab — Navigate between all interactive elements in logical document order.
  • Enter / Space — Activate buttons, links, and form controls.
  • Arrow keys — Navigate within composite widgets (e.g., mobile menu accordion).
  • Escape — Closes open menus and overlays.
  • Focus indicators — All interactive elements display a visible cyan focus outline (focus-visible) that meets the WCAG 2.1 AA contrast requirement. We do not suppress focus styles.
  • Logical focus order — Tab order follows the visual reading order of each page. Focus is never lost or trapped in an unexpected element.

No time limits are imposed on keyboard interactions. No features require simultaneous keystrokes beyond standard Tab and Enter usage.

// 06 — visual design & colour contrast

Contrast ratios that exceed the standard.

We have designed our colour palette with accessibility as a primary constraint. Key contrast measurements:

Primary Text (#eef0f8 on #070910)

Contrast ratio: 19.7:1 — exceeds WCAG AA (4.5:1) and AAA (7:1) requirements for normal text.

Secondary Text (rgba(200,212,232,0.62) on #070910)

Effective contrast ratio: approximately 5.1:1 — passes WCAG AA (4.5:1) for normal text. We are working to improve this further toward AAA where layout permits.

Accent / Cyan (#00E5FF on #070910)

Contrast ratio: 15.4:1 — significantly exceeds WCAG AAA requirements. Used for links, interactive indicators, and focus rings.

Colour is never used as the only means of conveying information. All states (error, success, active) include a textual or structural cue in addition to colour. Text is resizable to 200% browser zoom without loss of content or functionality.

// 07 — images & media

Alternative text and media standards.

  • Informative images — All images that convey content include descriptive alt text that communicates the purpose of the image in context.
  • Decorative images — Images used purely for visual decoration are marked with an empty alt="" attribute so screen readers skip them.
  • Logo — The VardWeb logo includes alt text "VardWeb" on all pages.
  • No autoplay — We do not use any auto-playing audio or video. No content flashes more than three times per second (WCAG 2.3.1).
  • Third-party embeds — Where third-party content is embedded, we include accessible labelling. We cannot guarantee the accessibility of third-party content itself.

// 08 — forms & interactive elements

Forms that work for everyone.

  • Labels — All form fields include programmatically associated labels via for/id pairing. Toggle groups use fieldset/legend for semantic grouping. No field is identified by placeholder text alone.
  • Error identification — Errors are described in text, not only by colour. Error messages identify the specific field affected and describe what is required to resolve the issue.
  • No time limits — No forms or interactive elements impose session time limits that would prevent users with disabilities from completing tasks.
  • ARIA on custom controls — Custom interactive components (mobile accordion, desktop dropdown, FAQ accordion) use WAI-ARIA roles, states, and properties including aria-expanded, aria-controls, aria-haspopup, and role=menu.
  • Status messages — Form submission success and error responses are announced to screen readers via aria-live="polite" regions, without requiring visual focus change.
  • Touch targets — All interactive elements on mobile meet a minimum touch target size of 44×44 CSS pixels, consistent with WCAG 2.5.5 (AAA target) and platform guidelines.

// 09 — known limitations

No known limitations remain as of June 2026.

We believe in transparent disclosure over vague assurances. All previously documented gaps have been fully remediated:

✅

All previously documented limitations have been resolved ahead of the Q3 2026 target:

  • Desktop dropdown navigation — Fully ARIA-compliant with aria-haspopup, aria-expanded, aria-controls, and role=menu on all pages (June 2026)
  • Reduced motion preference — prefers-reduced-motion CSS and JavaScript RAF guards implemented site-wide (June 2026)
  • Skip navigation link — Added to all 31 pages; visible on first Tab press, moves focus to main content (June 2026)
  • Decorative SVG labelling — All decorative inline SVGs carry aria-hidden="true" across all pages (June 2026)
  • Form status messages — aria-live="polite" regions added; screen readers announce success and error states (June 2026)
  • UI component contrast — Input borders and social button borders raised to meet 3:1 non-text contrast threshold (June 2026)

// 10 — assessment approach

How we test for conformance.

VardWeb assesses accessibility through a combination of automated and manual methods:

Automated Tools

We use the following tools to identify and monitor accessibility issues across our sites: axe-core (browser extension and CI integration), WAVE Web Accessibility Evaluator, Google Lighthouse Accessibility Audit, and Colour Contrast Analyser for manual contrast verification.

Manual Testing

Automated tools identify approximately 30–40% of all accessibility issues. We supplement automated testing with manual keyboard-only traversal of all pages, screen reader testing using NVDA (Windows) and VoiceOver (macOS/iOS), and review of all interactive component ARIA semantics against WAI-ARIA Authoring Practices Guide 1.2.

Self-Assessment

This statement is based on a self-assessment conducted by VardWeb. We have not engaged an independent third-party accessibility auditor for the current assessment cycle. We intend to commission an external audit in 2027.

// 11 — feedback & contact

Your feedback makes us better.

If you experience an accessibility barrier on vardweb.ca or vardweb.com, we want to know about it. Please be as specific as possible — the page, the element, your assistive technology and browser, and the nature of the barrier. We respond to all accessibility feedback within 2 business days and aim to resolve substantiated issues within 30 days.

When reporting an issue, please include:

  • The URL of the affected page on vardweb.ca or vardweb.com
  • A description of the barrier and the task you were trying to complete
  • The assistive technology and browser you are using (if applicable)
  • Your preferred format for a response

// 12 — formal complaints & escalation

If we don't resolve it, here's where to go.

We take all accessibility complaints seriously. If you have reported an issue to us and are not satisfied with our response, you have the right to escalate to the relevant regulatory body.

Ontario Residents (AODA): You may file a complaint with the Accessibility Directorate of Ontario at ontario.ca/accessibility.

Human Rights Complaints (Canada): Complaints relating to disability-based discrimination in accessing our services may be made to the Ontario Human Rights Commission or the Canadian Human Rights Commission.

EU Users (EAA): From June 2025, VardWeb's digital services are subject to the European Accessibility Act (EAA / EN 301 549) for EU users. Complaints may be directed to the competent authority in your EU member state.
