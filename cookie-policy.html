// 01 — what are cookies

Small files. Big transparency.

Cookies are small text files that a website places on your device — computer, phone, or tablet — when you visit. They are stored by your browser and sent back to the originating website on subsequent visits. Cookies allow websites to remember information about you, such as your preferences or authentication state.

Session Cookies vs. Persistent Cookies

Session cookies are temporary. They exist only while your browser is open and are automatically deleted when you close it. They contain no persistent identifying information.

Persistent cookies remain on your device after the browser is closed, for a defined duration. They allow a site to remember you across visits — for example, keeping you logged in or remembering your preferences.

First-Party vs. Third-Party Cookies

First-party cookies are set directly by VardWeb (vardweb.ca / vardweb.com). Only we can read them.

Third-party cookies are set by external services that provide functionality on our sites — such as analytics tools. These cookies are governed by those third parties' privacy policies, not ours.

// 02 — why we use cookies

Every cookie has a reason. Here's each one.

We use cookies for a narrow set of legitimate purposes. We do not use cookies to build advertising profiles, fingerprint devices, or sell data. Our cookie usage falls into four categories, only two of which require your consent:

  • Strictly Necessary — Required for the site to function. No consent needed under Canadian, EU, and UK law.
  • Functional — Remember your preferences to improve your experience. Consent can be withdrawn at any time.
  • Analytics — Anonymized, aggregate data about how visitors use the site. Consent can be withdrawn at any time.
  • Marketing — We do not use marketing or advertising cookies. Period.

// 09 — managing your preferences

You are always in control.

You have the right to accept, reject, or withdraw consent for non-essential cookies at any time. There are several ways to exercise this right:

  • Browser settings — The most reliable method. Configure your browser to block or delete cookies at any time (see Section 10 for browser-specific instructions).
  • Google Analytics opt-out — Install the GA Opt-out Add-on to block Google Analytics tracking across all sites.
  • Contact us — Email support@vardweb.com to request that any data associated with your session be deleted.

Blocking strictly necessary cookies will prevent certain features from functioning correctly. All other cookie categories can be disabled without affecting your ability to browse the site.

// 10 — browser controls

How to manage cookies in your browser.

All major browsers allow you to view, delete, and block cookies. Instructions for the most common browsers:

Google Chrome

Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data. You can block all cookies, block third-party cookies, or clear cookies for specific sites.

Mozilla Firefox

Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data. Firefox also offers Enhanced Tracking Protection (ETP), which blocks known third-party trackers automatically.

Apple Safari

Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data. Safari blocks cross-site tracking cookies by default. You can block all cookies or manage site-specific data.

Microsoft Edge

Settings → Cookies and site permissions → Cookies and site data. Edge includes Tracking Prevention with three levels (Basic, Balanced, Strict).

Note: Blocking or deleting strictly necessary cookies — including session and authentication cookies — may prevent certain features of the site from working correctly, including the client login portal and contact form submissions.

// 11 — do not track & global privacy control

We respect your browser's privacy signals.

Do Not Track (DNT)

Some browsers transmit a "Do Not Track" (DNT) signal to websites. Because there is no consistent technical standard for how DNT signals should be interpreted, our sites do not currently modify their cookie behaviour in response to DNT headers. We encourage you to use your browser's native cookie controls (Section 10) as the most reliable opt-out mechanism.

Global Privacy Control (GPC)

The Global Privacy Control (GPC) is an emerging browser signal that communicates your preference not to have your data sold or shared. Where technically feasible and legally required — including under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and its amendment (CPRA) — VardWeb honours the GPC signal by suppressing non-essential analytics cookies. We are committed to expanding GPC support as implementation standards mature.
